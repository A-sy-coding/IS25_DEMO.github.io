TSPT-VC: Feature Disentanglement with Timbre Self-Perturbed Transformation and Implicit Duration Guidance for Voice Conversion

Sang-Young An1, Nam-Seok Song2 and Joon-Hyuk Chang†

Department of Artificial Intelligence Application Hanyang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea 1
Department of Electronic Engineering, Hanyang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea 2, †
tbvjzsk@hanyang.ac.kr1,    insang99@hanyang.ac.kr2,     jchang@hanyang.ac.kr†

Abstract


Separating linguistic content, timbre, pitch, and rhythm in voice conversion remains challenging due to feature entanglement, which degrades synthesized speech quality and impairs the accurate transfer of the target speaker's identity. To address this issue, we propose TSPT-VC, a novel framework integrating three key components: (1) a timbre self-perturbed transformation module for enhanced timbre extraction, (2) an implicit duration guidance module for improved rhythm modeling, and (3) a cascade SSL content encoder for better linguistic information preservation. Experimental results, including objective metrics and mean opinion score (MOS) evaluations, demonstrate that TSPT-VC outperforms baseline models in timbre transfer, content preservation, pitch accuracy, and rhythm consistency. Furthermore, it produces more natural speech and achieves greater similarity to the target speaker, highlighting its effectiveness in voice conversion.
